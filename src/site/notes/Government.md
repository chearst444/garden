Johnson City’s municipal government is run by a 5-member Board of Commissioners who are all elected "at-large", meaning they represent the entire city as a whole rather than specific individual neighborhoods or city districts. 

Elections & Terms: Commissioners are non-partisan and serve four-year staggered terms. Local elections are held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November of even-numbered years, aligning directly with the state general election. 

# Johnson City Board of Commissioners

The Board of Commissioners serves as the central legislative and governing body for the City of Johnson City, Tennessee. Operating under a council-manager form of government, the Board is responsible for establishing municipal policies, passing local ordinances, voting on resolutions, and approving the annual balanced budget.

## Meeting Schedule & Administration
* **When:** Meets on the first and third Thursday of every month at 6:00 PM.
* **Where:** Municipal and Safety Building, 601 E. Main St., Johnson City, TN.
* **Primary Contact:** Executive Assistant Beth Greene (423-434-6002 | bgreene@johnsoncitytn.org).

## Core Governance Responsibilities
* **Budgetary Oversight:** Reviews and approves the City's annual fiscal budget following extensive work sessions between May and June.
* **Legislative Action:** Enacts city ordinances (requiring multiple readings for passage) and civil regulations.
* **Appointive Powers:** Appoints community members to municipal volunteer boards, including the Board of Zoning Appeals (BZA), Regional Planning Commission, and the Civil Service Commission.
