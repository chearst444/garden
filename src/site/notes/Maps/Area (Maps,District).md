To clarify how the city handles its divisions: Johnson City itself doesn't actually use an 11-district legislative system. Instead, the city's municipal government is run by a 5-member Board of Commissioners who are all elected "at-large", meaning they represent the entire city as a whole rather than specific individual neighborhoods or city districts.

 comes down to one of two other maps that cut through town:

1. The Washington County Commission Districts: When it comes to county-level representation, Washington County is split into 15 total districts. Because Johnson City is the largest population hub, several of those county districts (specifically Districts 6 through 11) blanket the city limits to determine who represents you on the county commission.

2. The Johnson City School Zones: Because the city operates its own independent school district separate from the county, the map is broken down by individual elementary school zones (like South Side, Mountain View, and Towne Acres).
