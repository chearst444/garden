## The Washington County Commission Districts: 
1. When it comes to county-level representation, Washington County is split into ==15 total districts== . Because Johnson City is the largest population hub, several of those county districts (specifically Districts 6 through 11) blanket the city limits to determine who represents you on the county commission.

## The Johnson City School Zones:
Because the city operates its own independent school district separate from the county, the map is broken down by individual elementary school zones (like South Side, Mountain View, and Towne Acres).  

## The Official Mapping Hub: 
To explore property boundaries, zoning layers, and neighborhood designations, check out the [Johnson City GIS Map Gallery](https://www.johnsoncitytn.org/departments/information_technology/gis/map_gallery.php).

## Future Development Lines: 
To look directly at how the new placetypes and growth boundaries are drawn for the next twenty years, you can use the [Johnson City Horizon 2045 Land Use Application](https://datahub-jctngis.opendata.arcgis.com/).
