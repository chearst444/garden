- **Johnson City, TN / Washington County** **Action**: Extended I-2 Industrial Moratorium (freeze extended to Dec 3, 2026) **Reason**: Noise/vibration-sensitive uses; 24/7 low-frequency noise impacting residential areas and landmarks like Ashley Academy. **Framework Used**: Commissioned a specialized **$96,880 JPM Acoustics engineering study**across **10 local sites** to legally bulletproof Title 11 updates. Final report and ordinance revisions slated for public review in late summer/early autumn. [View Moratorium →](https://www.wjhl.com/news/local/washington-county-commission-approves-data-center-moratorium/)

- **Limestone / Jonesborough, TN / Washington County** **Action**: Amended Ordinance & 1,500-ft Buffer **Reason**: Noise/vibration, residential proximity, nighttime noise, and human-perceptible vibration concerns. **Framework Used**: Passed strict ordinance banning facilities within 1,500 feet of residential structures, forcing developer-specific rezoning. [View Ordinance →](https://www.wjhl.com/news/jonesborough-board-amends-data-center-ordinance-and-discusses-fiscal-year-budgeting/)

- **Loudon County, TN / East Tennessee** **Action**: 6-Month Emergency Moratorium **Reason**: Infrastructure strain, baseline noise, and unmitigated environmental impact. **Framework Used**: Complete freeze on development permits to allow the planning commission to execute a comprehensive land-use study. [View Details →](https://www.wvlt.tv/2026/06/30/loudon-county-approves-pause-data-centers/)

- **Anderson County, TN / East Tennessee** **Action**: 2-Year Development Halt **Reason**: Need for localized impact data, expert testimony, and regional alignment. **Framework Used**: Extended moratorium freezing data center and crypto mining applications to gather independent expert assessments. [View Proposal →](https://www.wsmv.com/2026/06/16/anderson-county-commission-proposes-2-year-moratorium-local-data-centers/)

- **San Marcos, TX / Home-rule municipality** **Action**: Total Municipal Zoning Ban **Reason**: Threats to local water security and community-wide grid capacity depletion. **Framework Used**: Leveraged Home Rule authority to amend zoning laws, making data centers an ineligible use anywhere inside city limits. [View Ban →](https://www.texastribune.org/2026/06/30/texas-san-marcos-data-center-ban-zoning-laws/)
- **Monterey Park, CA / Los Angeles County** **Action**: Permit Moratorium **Reason**: Public backlash regarding rapid, unreviewed hyperscale industrial expansion. **Framework Used**: Enacted a temporary moratorium to reassess municipal regulations and establish community protections. [View Coverage →](https://www.route-fifty.com/artificial-intelligence/2026/06/data-center-moratoriums-gain-ground-states-and-cities/414090/)

- **Prince William County / Gainesville, VA (Digital Gateway)** **Action**: Project officially cancelled — QTS Realty Trust withdrew final Virginia Supreme Court appeal on July 2, 2026. **Reason**: Intense local/community pushback over location adjacent to historic Manassas National Battlefield Park and Occoquan watershed (historical preservation, environmental risks, skyrocketing residential power bills); procedural legal defects in original rezoning. **Framework Used**: Virginia Court of Appeals voided 2023 county rezoning approvals due to failure to separate newspaper public notices by the mandated six days. County dropped defense after spending $1.72M in taxpayer funds. Co-developer Compass Datacenters pulled out in May 2026. QTS abandoned the final appeal without a cost-sharing partner for utility upgrades. Part of broader national trend (30–50% of planned 2026 U.S. data center capacity facing delays/cancellations due to grid bottlenecks, opposition, and regulations). **Details**: Planned 2,100-acre site with up to 37 buildings / 22 million sq ft and 3–6 GW power demand (roughly equal to current Northern Virginia data center consumption).
