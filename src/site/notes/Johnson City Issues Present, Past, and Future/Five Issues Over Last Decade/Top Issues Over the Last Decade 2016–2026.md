# 1  [[Johnson City Issues Present, Past, and Future/Five Issues Over Last Decade/The Affordable Housing and Rental Crunch/Affordable Housing and Rental Crunch\|Affordable Housing and Rental Crunch]]

Driven by a post-pandemic population influx and high desirability rankings, Johnson City's real estate landscape became highly competitive. Housing assessments revealed a rental vacancy rate hovering near a critical 0.7% (compared to a 6.6% national average), leaving working-class families heavily rent-burdened and fueling high-profile municipal battles over the redevelopment of downtown low-income housing options like the John Sevier Center.  

# 2  [[Johnson City Issues Present, Past, and Future/Five Issues Over Last Decade/Downtown Revitalization and Major Flood Mitigation/Downtown\|Downtown]]

Over the last ten years, Johnson City successfully reshaped its urban core by addressing its historical Achilles' heel: downtown flooding. By investing heavily in stormwater infrastructure and creating intentional green spaces like Founders Park and King Commons, the city turned a flood-prone railroad district into a safe, walkable economic engine for local businesses.

# 3 [[Johnson City Issues Present, Past, and Future/Five Issues Over Last Decade/The West Walnut Street Corridor Reconstruction/West Walnut Street Corridor Reconstruction\|West Walnut Street Corridor Reconstruction]]

One of the largest infrastructure undertakings in recent city history, this multi-year, multi-million-dollar complete-streets overhaul targeted the crucial corridor linking downtown Johnson City to East Tennessee State University (ETSU). The decade-long focus involved completely replacing aging underground utilities, enhancing pedestrian safety, and laying the groundwork for private commercial investment along the strip.

# 4 [[Regional Healthcare Restructuring Impact\|Regional Healthcare Restructuring Impact]]

The 2018 merger of Mountain States Health Alliance and Wellmont Health System to form Ballad Health fundamentally altered Johnson City’s economic and medical landscape. As the home of the region’s primary Level 1 Trauma Center (Johnson City Medical Center), the city has spent the decade navigating systemic shifts in rural healthcare access, culminating in massive expansions like the recent $72 million emergency department modernization.  

# 5 [[Johnson City Issues Present, Past, and Future/Five Issues Over Last Decade/Outdoor Recreation and Tourism Infrastructureg/Outdoor Recreation and Tourism Infrastructure\|Outdoor Recreation and Tourism Infrastructure]] 

Capitalizing on the region's natural geography, the city spent the last ten years aggressively expanding its park and trail network. Anchored by the massive success and continued eastward expansion of the Tweetsie Trail, Johnson City successfully rebranded itself as a prominent regional hub for outdoor tourism, cycling, and retirement living.
