Access Gap: If a service worker downtown or on West Walnut wraps up a shift at a newly renovated restaurant or shop at 8:00 p.m., the lack of standard evening transit creates a severe barrier to mobility. While specialized work access services do exist within the transit system, a portion of the workforce is  unaware that these options are available to them or find the process of navigating the application and scheduling system too intimidating to handle. Instead of forcing workers to navigate the hassle of specialized request systems, simply extending the regular bus routes on their standard schedules would provide a seamless, transparent, and universally accessible solution for the entire community.
 
- **The Mobility Barrier:** If a family wants to take their kids to the new $37 million aquatic center in the evening, but they do not own a reliable vehicle, the early bus cutoff locks them out of a public resource paid for by community taxes.

Connecting Growth to Mobility

True progress means that investments in recreation and investments in the workforce move forward together. My argument does not oppose the city's growth; it asks the city to make sure **everyone can participate in that growth**. Providing an evening bus service ensures that these new amenities are truly for the entire public, not just those with private transportation.
