The most ironic logistical flaw in the entire $37 million [[Johnson City Issues Present, Past, and Future/Five Issues Current and near future/The FY2027 Budget and the Aquatics Center Tax Debate/Aquatics Center\|Aquatics Center]] project: **the city is building a regional tourist destination in a location that forces visitors to drive, while completely isolating local residents who rely on the bus.** [[1](https://wcyb.com/news/local/johnson-city-aquatic-center-stays-in-budget-but-changes-may-still-be-coming)]

 A massive geographical and transportation disconnect in how Johnson City is planning for the future.

1. The Total Lack of Nearby Hotels

- **The Location:** The aquatic center is being built at **Memorial Park Community Center** (off Bert Street and Cranberry Street). [[1](https://cms7files.revize.com/johnsoncity/Documents/Departments/Parks%20and%20Recreation/Final%20Johnson%20City%20Aquatics%20Feasibility%20Study-compressed_March%202026.pdf), [2](https://www.facebook.com/wjhlTV11/videos/news-channel-11s-jeff-keeling-is-learning-more-today-about-johnson-citys-propose/4473599266256294/)]

- **The Reality:** There are **zero hotels** within walking distance of Memorial Park. The city's primary hotel districts are miles away, concentrated off North Roan Street, the Bristol Highway, or near the retail hubs off I-26.

- **The Disconnect:** If the city’s main pitch is that the center will "bring in massive out-of-town swim tournaments and boost hotel tax revenue," every single out-of-town visitor, swim team, and family will be forced to rent cars or flood local roads to drive across town from their hotels. The project creates its own traffic problem. [[1](https://johnsoncitypress.com/news/377936/aquatic-center-can-be-a-boon-for-the-city/), [2](https://beverlypress.com/2022/11/planning-department-combs-through-tvc-2050/)]

2. The Current Bus Route Trap

- **The Map:** Memorial Park _is_ technically hooked into public transit right now. The **JCT Blue Route (Keystone)** stops right at Cranberry & Bert Street. [[1](https://www.johnsoncitytransit.org/stops/all_routes.pdf), [2](https://cms7files.revize.com/johnsoncity/Documents/Departments/Parks%20and%20Recreation/Final%20Johnson%20City%20Aquatics%20Feasibility%20Study-compressed_March%202026.pdf)]
- **The Trap:** Because that bus stops running at **6:15 p.m.**, local families and kids who want to use the facility after school, after work, or for evening open-swim sessions are entirely locked out if they don't own a car.

3. "Who Are We Actually Building This For?"

When you stack the lack of hotels next to the lack of evening transit, the big-picture contradiction is undeniable:

- If a visitor stays at a hotel on North Roan Street, they cannot take public transit to the aquatic center because the lines do not naturally connect them through evening hours.
- If a local resident lives in South Johnson City or near campus and wants to swim at 6:30 p.m., they cannot take the bus home because the system is already shut down for the night.

🔍 The Core Disconnect

 The Tourism Focus: The facility is explicitly funded and scaled to act as a major regional driver for sports tourism, meets, and out-of-town visitors arriving by car via major traffic corridors.

 The Transit Barrier: By prioritizing a highway-adjacent or auto-centric footprint over centralized municipal planning, the location creates a severe mobility barrier. It locks out the local families, students, and low-income residents who depend on the Johnson City Transit fixed-route bus lines for their daily recreation and community access.

 The Funding Contrast: While millions of dollars are being committed to this massive recreational capital asset, the regular city bus system remains heavily restricted to daytime operating hours, further compounding the isolation for transit-dependent citizens.
