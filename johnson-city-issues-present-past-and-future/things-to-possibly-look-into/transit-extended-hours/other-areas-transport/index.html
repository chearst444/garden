The current population of Boone, North Carolina is estimated to be ==between 19,900 and 20,100 residents==within the town limits.

Yes, Boone, North Carolina has a public transportation system called AppalCART, and it is completely fare-free. [1, 2]

The system acts as the transit authority for both the Town of Boone and greater Watauga County, heavily serving the Appalachian State University (ASU) campus, local apartment complexes, shopping districts, and downtown landmarks. [1, 2]

Operating Hours & Service Windows

Because the system is designed in conjunction with the university, schedules expand and contract based on whether ASU is in its regular semester or on a holiday break. [1, 2]

  • Standard Weekly Windows: Core fixed routes typically begin running around 6:35 a.m. and extend until 10:08 p.m. Monday through Friday. [1, 2]
  • Weekend Service: Weekend service scales down slightly. For example, major routes run on Saturdays from 9:05 a.m. to 5:34 p.m. and Sundays from 12:05 p.m. to 8:04 p.m.. [1]
  • Night Owl Routes: During peak Fall and Spring academic semesters, AppalCART deploys late-night "Night Owl" shuttles to accommodate students and late-shift workers. [1, 2]
  • Break Schedules: When university classes are not in session, the system moves to a reduced "Break Schedule," running fewer simultaneous buses per route and wrapping up earlier in the evening.
